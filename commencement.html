Chicanx/Latinx Commencement

Every June, Cal Poly holds a special commencement ceremony for Chicanx and Latinx students. Here are some Frequently Asked Questions to help inform you about the event!

What is Chicanx/Latinx Commencement (CLC)?

Chicanx/Latinx commencement is a graduation ceremony for Chicanx and Latinx-identifying students. There is one ceremony for everyone regardless of the college their major resides in, and it is a much smaller and more intimate ceremony than those for individual colleges, so you all get more recognition!

When is Chicanx/Latinx Commencement held?

Chicanx/Latinx Commencement takes place at the end of spring quarter, but there is no singular date that it falls on year after year. Best efforts are made to ensure that CLC does not conflict with any of the college commencements, although there is no guarantee as CLC typically is held in the Performing Arts Center and the space is used to host other events and performances. For updates on the date and time for your class' CLC, please keep in touch with La CASA on Instagram.

What do I need to do to participate in Chicanx/Latinx Commencement?

If you would like to participate in Chicanx/Latinx Commencement, there is an interest form that is sent out a couple months in advance where you can add your name to the list of graduates who will be participating. It is completely free for you to participate in and for your friends and family to attend, so we encourage everyone to be a part of it!

Will I receive a special stole for participating in CLC?

Yes, you will receive a stole as a participant in Chicanx/Latinx Commencement, as well as a special certificate and other small goodies!

I won't be participating in CLC, but may I still receive a stole?

Of course! When you fill out the interest form, there should be an option to mark that you will not be attending the ceremony, but that you would still like to receive the stole and other gifts.
